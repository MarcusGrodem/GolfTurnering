# Stavanger Student Golf Invitational

## Project Summary

The Stavanger Student Golf Invitational is a yearly, one-day golf and networking event for ambitious students and businesses in the Stavanger region. It combines an informal, fun golf tournament with structured opportunities for recruitment, professional networking, and community building.

The event is intended to strengthen the connection between Stavanger-area employers and students who study locally or have ties to the region. A central motivation is that many young adults leave Stavanger to study or work elsewhere. The event should help students discover relevant local opportunities and help businesses build relationships with prospective employees.

The event is not intended to generate a profit. Student fees and sponsor contributions should cover the full cost of running a high-quality event.

## Objectives

1. Help students build useful personal and professional networks.
2. Give businesses access to engaged students and prospective recruits.
3. Build a stronger community around students, employers, and Stavanger.
4. Create a recurring event that participants look forward to each year.
5. Deliver a fun experience where networking feels natural rather than forced.

## Target Participants

### Students

- Primarily students in business, finance, economics, and related fields.
- Primarily students from, studying in, or interested in working in the Stavanger area.
- Students pay an attendance fee.
- Golf equipment can be rented at the venue.
- The required level of prior golf experience has not yet been decided.

### Businesses

- Initial target sectors include banking, finance, consulting, and other relevant Stavanger employers.
- Businesses participate as sponsors and recruitment partners.
- Participating company representatives play in the scramble tournament without paying a player fee.
- Businesses can operate stands before the shotgun start and attend the post-tournament dinner and evening program.
- Their value proposition is direct access to a broad network of capable, eager students with connections to the Stavanger region.

## Event Format

- Held annually during the summer.
- One-day event with daytime golf followed by dinner and an evening afterparty.
- 18-hole tournament.
- Shotgun start.
- Scramble format with teams of three or four players.
- Teams should include company representatives, with the exact student-to-company composition still to be determined.
- Results include both gross and net scoring.
- The tone should be informal enough to be fun and social, while maintaining a professional standard suitable for recruitment and networking.

## Competitions and Prizes

Two main team prizes:

- Best gross score.
- Best net score.

Additional smaller competitions may include:

- Closest to the pin on one or more par-3 holes.
- Longest drive.
- Straightest drive.

Prize sourcing should prioritize sponsor-provided products, experiences, or gift cards to limit event costs and give sponsors additional visibility.

## Networking Experience

Networking should be built into the day without making the event feel like a formal career fair.

Potential touchpoints include:

- Company stands and introductions before the shotgun start.
- Mixed student and company scramble teams.
- Conversations throughout the round.
- Dinner with intentional seating arrangements.
- Prize ceremony and sponsor recognition.
- Informal afterparty.

## Website Vision

The website serves both event administration and year-round discovery between students and companies.

### Student Accounts

Students should be able to:

- Create and maintain a profile.
- Upload a profile photo.
- State their field of study and current year of study.
- Add areas of professional and academic interest.
- State their current study location and where they are originally from.
- Add education, skills, work experience, and other relevant experience.
- Add their golf handicap for tournament planning and team allocation.
- Upload a CV and potentially other supporting documents.
- View participating company profiles and opportunities.
- Register for the event and manage relevant participation details.

The student profile should therefore include at least:

- Full name and profile photo.
- Field of study.
- Current year of study.
- University or educational institution.
- Study location.
- Place of origin.
- Areas of interest.
- Skills and relevant experience.
- Golf handicap.
- CV and optional supporting documents.

### Company Accounts

Companies should be able to:

- Create and maintain a company profile.
- Upload a company logo and relevant company image.
- Present company information, employees, roles, and relevant opportunities.
- Select up to five core qualities they look for in students and prospective employees.
- Choose core qualities from suggested options or add their own custom qualities.
- View and search student profiles.
- Open student profiles and access documents that the student has chosen to share with verified companies.
- Manage company representatives participating in the event.

Each participating company representative should have a personal profile containing at least their name, role, profile photo, and golf handicap. The handicap is used for tournament administration and team allocation rather than recruitment matching.

Suggested core qualities may include:

- Analytical.
- Collaborative.
- Curious.
- Initiative-taking.
- Structured.
- Commercially minded.
- Adaptable.
- Clear communicator.
- Responsible.
- Relationship-oriented.

Custom and suggested qualities should use the same structured format so they can later support profile search, filtering, and matching. Students should be able to see which five qualities each company prioritizes.

### Access and Privacy

- Public visitors must not be able to access private student documents.
- Student documents must only be accessible to authenticated, authorized company users.
- Students should control which documents are shared and be told clearly who can access them.
- Company accounts should be verified before receiving access to student documents.
- The platform will require privacy notices, consent management, document-access controls, retention rules, and account/document deletion processes.
- The precise visibility of student profile fields to other students has not yet been decided.

## Financial Model

The event should operate at approximately zero profit. Student fees and sponsorship income should cover all event costs, including the free participation of company representatives.

### Expected Income

- Student participation fees.
- Tiered company sponsorship packages.
- Optional headline or main-event sponsor.
- In-kind sponsorship for prizes, equipment, food, transport, or event services.

### Expected Costs

- Green fees and course hire.
- Shotgun-start or tournament administration fees.
- Dinner, refreshments, and afterparty.
- Venue or room hire for the evening program.
- Golf equipment rental support, if subsidized.
- Prizes not supplied by sponsors.
- Website hosting, storage, email, and payment processing.
- Branding, signs, scorecards, name badges, and company stands.
- Photography or video.
- Insurance, permits, security, or medical readiness where required.
- Staff, volunteers, transport, and contingency.

### Budget Formula

The required student fee should be calculated after sponsor commitments:

`student fee = (total costs - cash sponsorship - other income) / number of paying students`

The working budget should include a contingency of approximately 10 percent. Any surplus should be retained for the following year's event, used to improve the student experience, or returned through reduced future fees rather than distributed as profit.

## Sponsorship Concept

Packages may be structured around clear participation and visibility benefits, for example:

- Main event partner.
- Dinner or afterparty partner.
- Hole or competition sponsor.
- Standard company partner.
- In-kind prize or service partner.

Potential benefits include company representatives in the tournament, a pre-start stand, company profile visibility on the website, access to opted-in student profiles, dinner attendance, branding at sponsored holes or competitions, and recognition during the prize ceremony.

Access to student data must not be sold as unrestricted access. It should depend on student consent, company verification, clear purpose limitations, and appropriate privacy controls.

## Measures of Success

- Number and quality of student registrations.
- Number of participating and returning companies.
- Student and company satisfaction.
- Useful introductions, interviews, internships, or job opportunities created.
- Sponsor renewal rate.
- Financial break-even performance.
- Growth in recognition and demand from year to year.
- A healthy balance between golf, networking, and fun.

## Open Decisions

The following details need to be resolved before producing an operational plan and detailed budget:

1. Event name and brand identity.
2. Target year, preferred month, and candidate golf course.
3. Maximum number of players and expected number of teams.
4. Number of free representatives included with each sponsorship package.
5. Student eligibility and whether recent graduates can participate.
6. Required golf experience, handicap requirements, and beginner support.
7. Handicap and net-scoring method for the scramble format.
8. Team allocation rules and desired company-to-student ratio.
9. Dinner and afterparty guest policy, alcohol policy, and minimum age.
10. Website profile visibility, document consent, retention period, and moderation responsibilities.
11. Legal organizer, payment handling, cancellation policy, and treatment of any surplus or loss.
12. Expected student fee range and target sponsor contribution.

## Working Principles

- Keep the student experience affordable and valuable.
- Make company participation useful without letting recruitment dominate the event.
- Design networking into shared activities rather than relying only on formal presentations.
- Protect student privacy and make document sharing transparent and voluntary.
- Prefer repeatable operations and sponsor relationships that support an annual event.
- Measure success through relationships and participant value, not profit.
